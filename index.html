I'm a postdoctoral researcher interested in the application of natural language processing and machine learning in educational technologies for foreign language learners.

  • Louvain-la-Neuve, Belgium
  • itec — imec research group at KU Leuven · CENTAL at UCLouvain
  • Contact

I started working as a research assistant funded by the Altissia e-learning company. I worked on a project focused on the automated grading of short answers (ASAG) written by English learners with CEFR proficiency levels. During this project, I collected the CEFR-ASAG dataset, which can be downloaded and used for non-commercial purposes.

In 2016, I started working as an F.R.S.-FNRS research fellow

During my doctorate, I was also involved in the ANR Alector project (2017-2020) on automatic text simplification for French children with reading disabilities and dyslexia. In Jul-Aug 2018, I was a visiting researcher at the Laboratoire Parole et Langage (LPL-CNRS, Aix-Marseille Université) where I worked on the project with Prof. Dr. Núria Gala. During this research stay, I focused on the identification of difficult words in the Alector corpus by aligning manual lexical simplifications with reading errors. Part of this work has been published in our LREC2020 paper.
